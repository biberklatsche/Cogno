# Code of Conduct

Cogno is intended to be a respectful, practical, and welcoming project for
people with different backgrounds and experience levels.

## Expected Behavior

- Be respectful and constructive.
- Assume good intent, but take responsibility for the impact of your words.
- Keep discussions focused on the project, the code, and the user experience.
- Give actionable feedback and explain technical concerns clearly.
- Be patient with people who are learning or who are new to the project.

## Unacceptable Behavior

Unacceptable behavior includes:

- Harassment, insults, threats, or personal attacks
- Discriminatory language or behavior
- Sexualized language or imagery
- Trolling, repeated disruption, or bad-faith arguments
- Publishing private information without permission
- Any behavior that would make the project unsafe or hostile for others

## Reporting

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, please contact the
maintainer privately. Do not escalate personal conflicts in public issues or
pull requests.

Reports should include relevant context, links, screenshots, or other details
that help the maintainer understand what happened. Reports will be reviewed as
fairly and confidentially as possible.

## Enforcement

The maintainer may take action to protect the project and its community. This
can include warnings, comment moderation, closing issues or pull requests,
temporary restrictions, or permanent bans from project spaces.

## Scope

This code of conduct applies to Cogno project spaces, including issues, pull
requests, discussions, community channels, and events connected to the project.
